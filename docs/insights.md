# Business Insights

These insights are based on the generated retail KPI exports in `data/processed/dashboard_exports/`.

## Executive Summary

- Total revenue reached `$2.60M` across `875` cleaned orders and `120` customers.
- Average order value was `$2,967.31`, with gross margin at `32.21%`.
- Refund exposure was `$175,293.94`, with a return rate of `8.34%`.

## Sales Performance

- December 2025 was the strongest month, generating `$266,881.47` in revenue.
- April 2025 was the weakest month, generating `$151,180.80` in revenue.
- The largest positive monthly movement happened in May after the April dip, showing a recovery opportunity worth investigating.

## Product and Category Performance

- Grocery and Electronics were the strongest revenue categories, each generating around `$645K`.
- Beauty had the strongest average gross margin at about `37.12%`.
- Office Supplies generated strong revenue but lower margin, which may require pricing or cost review.

## Customer Performance

- Revenue was balanced across Corporate, Consumer, and Small Business segments.
- Corporate customers generated the highest revenue at `$873,617.54`.
- The top customer generated `$48,548.07`, showing meaningful value concentration in high-spend accounts.

## Returns and Quality

- Beauty products with `Changed Mind` returns created the highest refund amount at `$20,188.71`.
- Grocery had refund exposure from both damaged and quality-related returns.
- Return-rate monitoring should be part of monthly reporting because returns affect revenue quality, not only sales volume.

## Recommendations

- Investigate the April revenue drop and identify whether it was caused by fewer orders, product mix, or customer segment behavior.
- Review Office Supplies margin because the category produces high revenue with weaker profitability.
- Track Beauty and Grocery returns more closely to separate product-quality issues from customer-preference returns.
- Use the dashboard monthly to monitor revenue, margin, top customers, and refund exposure together instead of looking at sales alone.
